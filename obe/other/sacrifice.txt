====== alay ======

===== Kahulugan: =====

Ang “handog”ay tumutukoy sa mga bagay na ibinibigay sa Diyos o sa diyus-diyosan. Ang alay ay may pagkakatulad sa handog pero kadalasan ito ay tumutukoy sa mga hayop na kinakatay at iniaalay sa Diyos o sa mga diyus-diyosan. Sa Bibliya ang mga handog at ang mga alay ay ibinibigay sa Diyos bilang pagsamba.

  * Iniuutos ng Diyos sa mga Israelita na maghanda ng mga alay at handog. Ginagawa rin ito para patawarin ng Diyos ang mga nagawa nilang kasalanan at para magpasalamat sa mga binibigay niyang pagpapala. Ang ilan sa mga ito ay “handog na sinusunog”, “handog na pambayad sa kasalanan”, “handog na pagkaing butil” at “handog na inumin.”
  * Ang tawag sa mga handog ay naaayon sa pangangailangan o kung para saan gagamitin.
  * Tanging ang dakila at walang kasalanang si Jesus na anak na Diyos lamang ang tuluyang makakapag-alis ng kasalanan ng mga tao. Hindi ito maaaring magawa sa pamamagitan ng pag-aalay ng mga hayop.

===== Translation Suggestions =====

  * "Offering" can be translated as "a gift to God" or "something given to God" or "something valuable that is presented to God" or "sacred gift."
  * "Sacrifice" can be translated as "to kill and animal and give it to God" or "meat presented to God" or "meat presented to an idol."

===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  * **[[:tl:obs:notes:frames:03-14|[03-14]]]** After Noah got off the boat, he built an altar and **sacrificed**  some of each kind of animal which could be used for a **sacrifice**. God was happy with the **sacrifice**  and blessed Noah and his family.
  * **[[:tl:obs:notes:frames:05-06|[05-06]]]**"Take Isaac, your only son, and kill him as a **sacrifice**  to me." Again Abraham obeyed God and prepared to **sacrifice**  his son.
  * **[[:tl:obs:notes:frames:05-09|[05-09]]]** God had provided the ram to be the **sacrifice**  instead of Isaac.
  * **[[:tl:obs:notes:frames:13-09|[13-09]]]** Anyone who disobeyed God's law could bring an animal to the Tent of Meeting as a **sacrifice**  to God. A priest would kill the animal and burn it on the altar. The blood of the animal that was **sacrificed**  covered the person's sin and made that person clean in God's sight.
  * **[[:tl:obs:notes:frames:17-06|[17-06]]]** David wanted to build a temple where all the Israelites could worship God and offer him **sacrifices**.
  * **[[:tl:obs:notes:frames:48-06|[48-06]]]** Jesus is the Great High Priest. Unlike other priests, he offered himself as the only **sacrifice**  that could to take away the sin of all the people in the world.
  * **[[:tl:obs:notes:frames:48-08|[48-08]]]** But God provided Jesus, the Lamb of God, as a **sacrifice**  to die in our place.
  * **[[:tl:obs:notes:frames:49-11|[49-11]]]** Because Jesus **sacrificed**  himself, God can forgive any sin, even terrible sins.
